After finishing my university studies in Romania (Electronics and Telecommunication, 5 years) I moved to Hungary where I joined Ericsson and worked in telecommunication for more than two decades.  On my journey I’ve experienced the development of telecommunication from fixed networks through 2G, 3G and 4G up to the 5G mobile networks.  There was never a dull moment – I helped implement the latest technologies in more than 20 countries, acquiring “on the fly” the new technological advancements.  In 2017 I moved to Luxembourg because of familial reasons, and I continued to work remotely for Ericsson in SW development with regular travelling back to Hungary.

Driving to Budapest (1200 km) needs 12 hours concentration, and I was dreaming about Self-Driving Cars, while trying to stay awake drinking coffee and listening to AI/ML and SDC podcasts.
I absolved countless online courses in the AI/ML domain with the intent to divert my career in this direction within Ericsson.  When the ML/AI path just opened within the company, the pandemic crossed my plans.  Travelling was not possible anymore, everybody started working remotely, but Ericsson enforced a new policy: remote working from abroad was prohibited.

To further enhance my expertise in AI/ML, I enrolled in the ISM (Interdisciplinary Space Master) program at the University of Luxembourg.  I've got my diploma this September, and I really enjoyed the possibility to get new expertise and enlarge my world view. On my SSP (Space Scientific Project) I worked on generating training images for lens flare removal using Blender's Python interface and my master thesis subject was testing of space software using LLMs.
I believe my diverse experiences in telecommunications, coupled with my recent studies in space technologies and AI/ML, make me a strong candidate for this PhD position. I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss my potential contribution to your research team in person.
